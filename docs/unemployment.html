Unemployment in U.S States

In this project I examine U.S states that registered highest and lowest unemployment rates in the first half of 2019. I went ahead to graph nonfarm labor productivity among U.S states.

MUSINGUZI BLANSHE

Published Octocer 12, 2019

Every month, President Trump brag about historical unemployment rates in the USA arguing that his administration deserves more credit than it receives for reducing unemployment.

Clustering states in groups of five states that had highest unemployment rate and states that had lowest unemployment rate, this article track states that registered highest unemployment reduction.

I sorted data and picked states that had highest and lowest unemployment from January to June. Highest unemployment: Alaska, District of Columbia, West Virginia, New Mexico and Arizona were the states with highest unemployment rates. But West Virginia stood out. Why? It registered a significant unemployment rate reduction from 5.2 to 4.4 percent.

The cluster of states that had lowest uneployment rates were; Hawaii, North Dakota, Vermont, New Hampshire, Iowa were the states with lowest unemployment rate. Vermont stood out in this cluster because its unemployment rate went down from 2.5 to 2.1 percent.

Unemployment in Iowa was static for the first half of 2019. The rate went up in Hawaii from February to June. The rate went up in New Hampshire in June. Unemployment went down in North Dakota in January and February but remained static during the four months that followed.
